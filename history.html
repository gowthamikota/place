History of the Isha Foundation

The Isha Foundation is a non-profit organization founded in 1992 by Sadhguru, with the aim of fostering human well-being and creating a conscious planet through various spiritual, social, and environmental initiatives. The foundation’s efforts have been focused on the inner transformation of individuals, as well as large-scale outreach programs to address societal issues.

In the early days of the foundation, Sadhguru's vision was to create a space where people could experience inner peace and connect with their true potential. This vision manifested with the establishment of the Isha Yoga Center in Coimbatore, India. This center became the foundation's focal point for offering programs and teachings designed to bring about personal growth through yoga and meditation. The Isha Yoga Center became a place where individuals could pursue spiritual growth and engage in a variety of initiatives aimed at addressing key issues affecting society.

In 1999, a significant milestone for the Isha Foundation occurred with the consecration of Dhyanalinga, a consecrated space that provides a unique environment for meditation. Dhyanalinga was designed to be an energy center that allows visitors to experience deep meditative states, offering a powerful atmosphere of spiritual rejuvenation. This sacred space marked a turning point for the foundation as it began to take on larger, more impactful projects that could reach people globally.

Another key milestone came in 2006 with the consecration of the Adiyogi Shiva statue. Standing at 112 feet, this statue became the largest bust statue in the world and is dedicated to the idea of human potential. The Adiyogi statue serves as a symbol of the first yogi and a reminder of humanity’s potential for growth and transformation. It has become an iconic figure, representing the essence of the Isha Foundation’s teachings about self-realization and spiritual growth.

In 2017, the Isha Foundation launched the Rally for Rivers campaign, a nationwide movement in India aimed at revitalizing the country’s rivers. The campaign focused on creating awareness about the deteriorating state of rivers in India and encouraged people to take action to save them. The foundation worked with environmentalists, policy makers, and citizens to push for sustainable practices that would restore the health of India’s river systems. The initiative emphasized the importance of water conservation and the need for urgent action to prevent further damage to the environment.

In 2020, the foundation continued to expand its global reach with numerous projects aimed at addressing social, environmental, and health issues. One of the key initiatives is Cauvery Calling, which focuses on rejuvenating the Cauvery River basin by planting trees and promoting agroforestry. This project aims to restore the natural balance of the region and bring about long-term ecological benefits. Additionally, Isha Foundation continues to focus on global causes like soil degradation and sustainable farming through movements like the Save Soil campaign. This global initiative aims to raise awareness about soil health and advocate for practices that protect soil and ensure food security for future generations.

Over the years, the Isha Foundation has evolved from a local yoga center to a global organization with millions of participants in its programs, outreach initiatives, and social movements. Its work spans a wide range of areas, from spiritual growth and yoga to environmental conservation and social responsibility. The foundation remains committed to its vision of creating a conscious planet and empowering individuals to live meaningful, transformative lives.
