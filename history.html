History of the Isha Foundation

The Isha Foundation was established in 1992 by Sadhguru. It is dedicated to human well-being and creating a conscious planet through spiritual and social initiatives.

Early Beginnings

The journey of Isha Foundation began with Sadhguru’s vision to create a platform for people to enhance their well-being through inner transformation. Starting with the establishment of the Isha Yoga Center in Coimbatore, India, the foundation has grown over the years to address a wide range of social, environmental, and spiritual issues.

Milestones

  • 1992: Isha Foundation was founded by Sadhguru in Coimbatore, India.
  • 1999: The consecration of Dhyanalinga, a unique meditative space.
  • 2006: Consecration of the Adiyogi Shiva statue, the largest bust statue in the world.
  • 2017: The launch of the Rally for Rivers campaign, aimed at revitalizing India’s rivers.
  • 2020: The foundation continues to expand with numerous social projects across the globe.

Initiatives

Isha Foundation has been instrumental in launching several initiatives that address crucial global issues. These include:

  • Rally for Rivers: A nationwide campaign to rejuvenate India's rivers.
  • Cauvery Calling: A project focused on reviving the Cauvery River basin through large-scale tree planting.
  • Project GreenHands: A tree-planting initiative that has already planted millions of trees across Tamil Nadu.
  • Save Soil: A global movement to raise awareness about soil degradation and promote sustainable farming practices.
